How it works

It's one loop, really.

You build your world once. After that, every session is the same rhythm — advance the clock, read what the world did, bring it to the table.

Author your world

Sketch settlements, factions and people. Give each one a goal, a fear, and a line it won't cross.

Advance the day

One tap moves time forward a day or a week. Every entity acts on its drives, against the others.

Read the chronicle

The world reports what happened and why, grouped by day, traceable back to the root cause.

Run it at the table

First moves, secrets and levers are laid out per entity. Bring prep that already happened.

Why it works this way

A real simulation underneath.

It's not a random generator and it's not a chatbot. It's a deterministic simulation — the same inputs always produce the same world — so it's something you can actually plan around. Here's what that gets you.

Deterministic, not random

Same seed, same world. Re-run a week or branch a decision and you get the same result — nothing changes unless you change it.

Entities with their own goals

Wants, Fears, Won't. Factions scheme and people act without you holding every string.

The chronicle remembers

Every beat keeps its cause. Follow any event back through the chain that made it happen.

Local-first & offline

Your worlds live on your machine as plain files. No account, no cloud, no telemetry.

✨

AI is optional flavour

Plain deterministic text by default. Turn on prose for colour — it never changes what happened.

Two themes, one desk

Ink by lamplight or Vellum by day. The whole workspace reskins with a single switch.

News & release notes

What's new.

Small updates land regularly. Most of what's here started as something missing at the table.

v1.4.0 Latest June 12, 2026

The Forecast

  • NewForecast view. Run the simulation forward speculatively, then tick the turns you want to keep and commit — the rest are discarded.
  • New"Will surface" panel guesses which secrets are about to break, and roughly when.
  • ImprovedThe story log now traces every beat back to its cause with a single why toggle.
  • FixedAdvancing a week no longer double-counts debts that came due mid-step. (This one annoyed me for months.)
v1.3.0 April 28, 2026

Sessions & scene notes

  • NewSessions screen. Drop quick scene notes during play, then promote any note into world-changing GM feedback when you advance time.
  • NewEntities can now carry an uploaded portrait — framed to match the initials avatar, with a graceful fallback.
  • ImprovedVellum theme contrast tuned for daylight reading.
v1.2.0 March 9, 2026

The Party ledger

  • NewParty view. Track where the PCs stand with every faction, plus a running ledger of who owes whom.
  • ImprovedRoster filtering is now instant across worlds with hundreds of entities.
  • FixedUndo now restores secret reveal-state correctly across an advance.

That's the whole thing.
